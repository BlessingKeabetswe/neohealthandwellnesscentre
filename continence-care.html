Conditions We Treat

Our specialists provide expert care for various types of urinary and fecal incontinence

Stress Incontinence

Leakage with coughing, sneezing, or physical activity due to weakened pelvic muscles.

Urge Incontinence

Sudden, intense urge to urinate followed by involuntary loss of urine (overactive bladder).

Mixed Incontinence

Combination of stress and urge incontinence symptoms.

Functional Incontinence

Physical or cognitive impairments that make reaching the toilet difficult.

Fecal Incontinence

Inability to control bowel movements, leading to involuntary stool leakage.

Post-Surgical Incontinence

Incontinence following prostate surgery, hysterectomy, or other pelvic procedures.
